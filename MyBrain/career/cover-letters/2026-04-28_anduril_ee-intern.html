Dear Anduril Hiring Team,

I'm applying for the Electrical Engineering Intern position (Summer 2026, Costa Mesa) at Anduril. I'm a Junior at UC San Diego pursuing a B.S. in Electrical Engineering with a Machine Learning and Controls track, returning to UCSD in fall to finish my final year. I'm a U.S. Citizen, eligible for export-controlled work without sponsorship.

What draws me to this role is that it sits exactly where I've been spending my time — at the boundary between hardware and software, where you can't ignore either side. In ECE 148 last quarter (Winter 2026), I worked through the full embedded stack on a Raspberry Pi: configuring I2C interfaces and udev USB permissions for an OAK-D Lite stereo camera, integrating a VESC motor controller over serial, patching library compatibility issues to make the open-source DonkeyCar framework work with our hardware, and then training and deploying a CNN behavior-cloning model on the same Pi. Across three individual labs (behavior cloning, GPS-based path following, ROS 2 integration) I got a real sense of how brittle the boundary between "device shows up in lsusb" and "the system actually drives" can be — and how much of an electrical engineer's job lives in that gap.

For my four-person final project (autonomous recycling collector), I owned the Python servo-control script and the Raspberry Pi system integration, while teammates handled YOLOv8 detection, ROS 2 state machine, and 3D-printed mechanics. Working across ECE / CompE / Aerospace / Mechanical taught me how much of "delivery" is making clean handoffs between disciplines — which from what I read is exactly how Anduril ships products.

My weak spots relative to the role are honest: I haven't used Altium for PCB design (only LTspice / PSpice for circuit simulation), and I haven't built production firmware. But I'm comfortable with embedded Linux internals, oscilloscope-level debugging from coursework, and I learn hardware tools quickly when there's a real system in front of me.

I'd be excited to spend a summer building electronics that ship to real customers under real constraints. Thank you for considering my application.

Sincerely,
